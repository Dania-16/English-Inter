Tongue Twisters

Here we'll present you some tongue twisters so you can learn new and random words. A tongue twister is a series of words or a longer piece, like a poem, constructed to be very difficult to pronounce properly. Tongue twisters are used to create humor by challenging someone to repeat them very fast and listening to the funny results, as well as by public speakers and speech language students to increase verbal agility.

T-T NUMBER 1

Can clams cram cans of candy?

Words that you may not know the meaning:

  • Clams: burrowing marine mollusk living on sand or mud; the shell closes with viselike firmness
    • Another definition could be dollar, dollar bill, one dollar bill, buck, clam (noun) a piece of paper money worth one dollar
  • Cram: To move into and fully occupy a space



T-T NUMBER 2

Slow feet, quick feet. Trick feet, sick feet. Up feet, down feet. Here come clown feet. Small feet, big feet. Here come pig feet. His feet, her feet. Fuzzy fur feet

Words that you may not know the meaning:

  • Fuzzy: Not clear; indistinct/ Not coherent; confused: a fuzzy plan of action.
  • Fur: he skin of certain animals, as the sable, ermine, or beaver, covered with such a coat.


T-T NUMBER 3

Betty Botter bought some butter. “But," said she, "this butter's bitter! If I put it in my batter, it will make my batter bitter. But a bit of better butter will but make my batter better." Then she bought a bit of butter, better than the bitter butter, made her bitter batter better. So 'twas better Betty Botter bought a bit of better butter!

Words that you may not know the meaning:

  • Batter: mixture of flour and liquid with other ingredients, such as leavening agents, shortening, sugar, salt, eggs, and various flavourings, used to make baked goods and other food products.
  • Bitter: Having or being a taste that is sharp, acrid, and unpleasant.
  • 'twas: contraction of it was.


T-T NUMBER 4

Six sleek swans swam swiftly southwards.

Words that you may not know the meaning:

  • Sleek:smooth and shiny/ Able to or designed to move efficiently through air or water;
  • Swiftly: At high speed; quickly.
  • Swam: to move through water using arms and legs or fins, tails etc .

Tongue T number 5

A tutor who tooted the flute Tried to tutor two tooters to toot Said the two to the tutor “Is it tougher to toot Or to tutor two tooters to toot?”

Words that you may not know the meaning:

Tood: to give or cause to give/ to make a short sound or series of short sounds. However, in the context would be the first definition.

Tongue T number 6

Silly Sally swiftly shooed seven silly sheep. The seven silly sheep Silly Sally shooed Shilly-shallied south. These sheep shouldn’t sleep in a shack; Sheep should sleep in a shed.

Words that you may not know the meaning:

Swiftly: Coming, occurring, or accomplished quickly/ Quick to act or react.
Shooed: frighten away animals or birds./ to scare, drive, or send away
Shallied: to show indecision or hesitation; vacillate.

Tongue T number 7

The greedy ape said as he ate, The greener green grapes are, The keener keen apes are To gobble green grape cakes, They’re great!

Words that you may not know the meaning:

Ape: any of various primates with short tails or no tail at all
Greedy: having or marked by an eager and often selfish desire especially for material possessions.
Kenner: Individual eager to demonstrate knowledge or participate enthusiastically in school, church, seminars, etc.

Tongue T number 8

She sells seashells on the seashore. The shells she sells are seashells, I’m sure. And if she sells seashells on the seashore, Then I’m sure she sells seashore shells.

Words that you may not know the meaning:

Seashells: a hard, protective outer layer usually created by an animal that lives in the sea. The shell is part of the body of the animal.
Seashore: land adjacent to the sea/ all the ground between the ordinary high-water and low-water marks.
