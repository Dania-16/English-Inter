Writing essays

Do you know the steps you need to take for writing an essay?

First of all you should have an idea of the assignment, since writing an essay is not just anything. In the first instance, consider the topic you are going to investigate and you should know that there are different types of essays, so the structure varies. In this section you will know how to elaborate an essay applying characteristics that include any type of these.

What's an essay?

It is often considered synonymous with a story or a paper or an article. Essays can be formal as well as informal. Formal essays are generally academic in nature and tackle serious topics. "Writing an academic essay means fashioning a coherent set of ideas into an argument. Because essays are essentially linear—they offer one idea at a time—they must present their ideas in the order that makes most sense to a reader. Successfully structuring an essay means attending to a reader's logic." (Harvard university).

There are seven things to consider before writing your essay: sender, receiver, message, channel, code, referent and context. Of these, the most important by far is your thesis, or the crux of what your essay is about.

Here's the explanation for each of the elements.



  • Issuer/Sender: That person who will be in charge of writing the text considering its subject of which it will deal. In addition, it is said that each one has a different style to write what you want to transmit or expose.

  • Receiver: Person who reads the essay when interested in that topic that is exposed, so that, depending on the type it is, it will be given an interpretation (subjective mode), for example if an essay was written on a play, or if an opinion or a text of reflection and critical part of a current event were included.

  • Message: This is the object of the communication. The Message contains the information that the Sender sends to the Receiver through a certain Channel or means of communication
  • Channel: Means by which the writing will be transmitted can be through a magazine, and can even be written to be recited. (Written or oral medium).

  • Code: Refers to the language that will be used to write. There are even essays that use two languages as in the case of academics where the summaries of the content are presented in different languages or the keywords to be used.
  • Referent: it is the real object to which the message of the communication refers and that is perceived by the receiver when decoding the message he receives from the sender. It is the theme around which the communicative process is elaborated.

  • Context: it is the environment that surrounds the communicative situation, the extralinguistic situation that surrounds the message. The situational context can be political, social, historical, etc.




Steps to follow in order to write a good essay

Step number 1:

First of all you should have a topic to write about it, as well as an idea of what it is or at least a general idea. After having a topic in mind, (consider that you are now committed to getting it right). make a list of the points you want to address or that are relevant so that you can include them in the essay. You can group the ideas based on how similar they are. so that you can form subtopics to address.

Step number 2:

Look for information on the subject, however, you must be careful when selecting information since remember that anyone can upload information to the web that is not true. Therefore, we recommend that you search PDF documents belonging to educational or international institutions. Remembering that the bibliography consulted must present characteristics such as: authorship, recent dates etc. Also, consider keywords from your research this will facilitate the search for what will make up your text.

Step number 3:

After you have read the selected sources, it is time to write an introduction, do not forget the format they ask you. In this first part of the text you can mention something that catches the reader, that is, mention a curious fact, make an analogy etc. Subsequently, an entry is given of what will be found in the text, that is, the theme, also indicating the purpose of the writing (to entertain, expose, to create awareness about something) and what is achieved from it.

Step number 4:

Start writing the corresponding subtopics based on the articles read. Here the connectors must be handled in order to generate the textual properties (coherence, adequacy, cohesion). That is, it is about adapting the text based on the audience to which it will be focused. And you should not forget to quote the authors of the other documents in case of paraphrasing and textual quotations, where after these it is recommended to make an analysis.

Step number 5:

Write on a piece of paper what has been learned with the development of the topic. And think about whether the topic can help generate reflection on something that is happening in the current context. Sort the ideas and pass them to the document. To finish, you should be given a grammar, spelling and punctuation check.




Now let's write a resumé

Fist of all... What is a resumé?A resume is a brief document that highlights an individual's experience, qualifications, and skills, in the hopes of securing a job interview. In some regions of the world, the term is used interchangeably with Curriculum Vitae (CV), while in other places, the two terms refer to very different documents. A resume is a summary of your knowledge, experience, and qualifications that will allow the recruiter to make a decision.


Points to consider on a resumé:


  1. Contact Information
  2. Opening Statement: Summary or Objective
  3. Work History
  4. Education
  5. Soft Skills and Technical Skills
  6. Certifications and Professional Memberships
  7. Achievements and Awards
  8. Additional Sections (Community Involvement, Volunteering, etc.)

The importance of a well written Professional Resume cannot be stressed upon enough. A Resume is a medium for advertising yourself, it’s the first means in which you use to present yourself as a job applicant and try to claim that you are the best choice candidate to a prospective employer. though that a Professional Resume is always an evolving document, which improves over the period of time, during your career, so keep modifying and improving on it after its first draft and keep its different versions with you as you move forward in your chosen career. Through a well-written resume, you can sell the skills which you have accumulated over these years. You can let the recruiter know what all skills that you have acquired through different jobs and how you are going to utilize them for the betterment of the prospective organization. This is exactly what recruiter need to know now-a-days. They want to know in which way your skills will prove beneficial for their organization. And if your resume is successful in telling them so, the purpose is served.
