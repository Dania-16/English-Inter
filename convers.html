Job Interview


Mss. Crow: Hello Ms. Brown, I’m Kristina Crow. Thanks so much for coming in.
Ms. Brown: It’s my pleasure, thanks for meeting with me.
Mss. Crow: Of course. Did you have any trouble by scheduling the appointment or finding this office.
Ms. Brown: Just a bit by scheduling but I could solve it. And the directions given for the office were clear. Thanks for asking.
Mss. Crow: Ok, so let’s get started. Why don’t you tell me something about yourself, I refer to working experience or your history in the area you studied.
Ms. Brown: I graduated from the chemical engineering career of the University of Cambridge, and I have worked in companies such as Nestlé, Coca Cola, occupying positions of line supervisor and quality mainly. I think they always choose me in those areas because of my great capacity for reflection and analysis. However, I am looking for a position higher than what I have been assigned in previous jobs.
Mss. Crow: Oh, that’s great! Can you tell me how you heard about this position?
Ms. Brown: Yes, in the first instance, it was rather because of an investigation in the job board. And, because some colleagues with whom I have worked have told me that I must look for an excellent work environment, and I think I could find it here.
Mss. Crow: What attracted you to our company?
Ms. Brown: I read that the job involved a lot of organization and interaction with chemical processes. And that there are overtime to practice in the administration area.
Mss. Crow: Yes, that’s true. linking what you mention, what would you say is your greatest strength?
Ms. Brown: I am a person who knows how to organize the ideas of different people to make them the same. And I know how to work under pressure, in addition to having an excellent capacity for analysis and observation.
Mss. Crow: That’s wonderful, and can you tell me how would you handle a situation where your boss or a coworker don’t agree with you in something?
Ms. Brown: I would first listen to the justification why he or she disagrees with my idea, but I would try to understand his or her opinion as well. And so we will probably come to an agreement.
Mss. Crow: What about your biggest weakness?
Ms. Brown: I think that I’m not too patient and sometimes that things don’t go as I wanted to.
Mss. Crow: Yes, that can happen. Anyways… do you have any question for me?
Ms. Brown: Would you call me if you are interested in me, or would it be via mail?
Mss. Crow: Both, but don’t worry about that. We will call you in the next three days.
Ms. Brown: Thank you for considering me for the position. Have a nice day!
